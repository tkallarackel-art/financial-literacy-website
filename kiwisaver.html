What are
Kiwisavers?

Kiwisavers are New Zealands retirement savings process established in 2007. It helps you save money for when you stop working. You automtically contribute part of your earnings to the Kiwisaver, your employer does too and sometimes even the goverment. The money in your KiwiSaver is invested so it can grow over time.

Your first
home

When purchasing your first home you may withdraw the money from your KiwiSaver in order to help pay for it but there are rules you have to follow. To withdraw it in the first place ususally you would have to need to have been a member for at least 3 years and you have to live in the property you plan to buy. You must also leave a minimum of $1000 and funds transferred from an Australian Superannuation scheme must also be untouched.

Common mistakes
people do

Many people regret not doing these things that could potentially benefit them in the future.

  1. Opting out early - long term commitment means more accumulating money
  2. Choosing a fund that is too safe (usually young peple) - "Riskier" plans often have long term benefits. In the short term they may decrease in value but over time they have show to have better reward. Playing it safe can lead to missing out on large benefits and less growth.
  3. Withdrawing money for non-essential reasons - withdrawing money for non-essential reasons can lead to less money when you need it.
